And if either of your parents nudges you about wanting grandchildren one more time ...    To be sure, the line between amicability and all-out war can be thin where in-laws are concerned.
With practice, though, experts say, you can learn to get along ... and the sooner the better.
Intruding in-laws are a mainstay on the marital trouble list, says Marlene Watson, a couples-and-family therapist in Philadelphia.
``Parents never stop being protective or wanting the best for their child,'' she says.
Typically, she explains, one partner may feel that an in-law is interfering and his or her spouse is not doing anything about it.
Then, to make matters worse, the spouse feels he or she is being attacked by the partner and pressured by one or both sets of parents.
